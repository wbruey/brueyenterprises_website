Coming and Going

Routine access to the appartment.

The doormen have been notified that you will be staying. Approach the front desk, identify yourself, and say that you are guests of Will Bruey and you want the keys. You will then be free to come and go as you please. You may need to re-identify yourself to other shifts of doormen. The apartment door has several locks on it. Way too much fault tolerance. Personally, I don't even lock the door. If there is a thief on the upper east side and he or she correctly guests the unlocked apartment door in a 42 story building after getting past the security personnel, then like, fine. That risk is way lower than the 800 dollars i had to drop to replace the lock when I locked myself out over the summer. But I digress. You will be given two keys, one with a black plastic top and a plain one. The plain metal key is for the dead bolt and the black key is for the handle lock. I'd highly recommend just using the deadbolt. No risk of locking yourself out, and the handle lock is complicated AF. It has like three settings, it's over engineered. I wouldn't touch the handle lock from the inside either. It owes me 800 bucks. When you are done your stay at the apartment, just lock the dead bolt and drop off the keys with the front desk.

What to do in the city.

Restaurants, Nightlife, and Daytime Fun

As mentioned in the previous section, there is a grocery store in the building at the 63rd St. and 3rd Ave. corner (just exit apt building and wrap around the building to the right and you can't miss it). There are a few good restaurants in the walking distance. The East Pole is good as is the Jeffrey. If you want something fast, there is a “Hale and Hearty” for soup and sandwiches. "Eat Here Now" is a good old fashioned diner on the block. There is a Le Pain for classy breakfast munchies. Other than that, you'll want to leave the upper east side. The best restaurants and nightlife are in the West (Greenwich) Village. The west side also has some good museums. Midtown has some tourist attractions. Not much is in the financial district except the World Trade Center observatory and the 9/11 memorial. Only go the memorial if you are emotionally prepared for like 3 hours of heart breaking sadness followed by existential dread for another 2 hours. Central Park walks are great during the day, it also has a great ice skating rink. The lower east side is where NYU is and has a lot of college town vibe. Hell’s Kitchen has all the theatre stuff near Broadway and some lively fun bars too. Chelsea, the West Village, and the meatpacking district is where you want to go out to party. Cool cocktail bars include "The Up and Up" and "The Blond". Text me for whatever you are in the mood for and we'll find the fun.
